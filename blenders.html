How to choose a blender

Power and jar design matter most, but the right blender also depends on what you make and how many people you feed.

Power and motor type

For frozen fruit, ice, and leafy greens, look for a motor rated around 1,000 watts or two horsepower. Lower-powered blenders are fine for soft fruit and protein shakes but will struggle and leave a gritty texture with tougher ingredients.

Full-size vs. personal

A full-size pitcher blender handles soups, big-batch smoothies, and family use. A personal blender like the NutriBullet Pro is cheaper, quicker to clean, and blends straight into a travel cup — perfect for solo daily drinks.

Cleaning and noise

Self-clean cycles and dishwasher-safe jars save real time. High-powered blenders are loud by nature, so if you blend early or in an open kitchen, check noise levels — a few quieter models exist, and we factor it into every rating.
