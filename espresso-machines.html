How to choose an espresso machine

Espresso machines range from press-and-go to fully manual. Here's how to land on the right level for you.

Decide how hands-on you want to be

If you enjoy dialing in grind size and tamping, a semi-automatic like the Gaggia Classic Pro rewards the effort. If you just want a reliable shot, an all-in-one with a built-in grinder and guided dosing takes most of the guesswork out.

Don't overlook the grinder

Fresh, consistent grounds matter more than almost anything else. Machines with an integrated grinder save money and counter space; if you choose one without, budget for a quality burr grinder to go alongside it.

Milk steaming matters

For lattes and cappuccinos, look at the steam wand. Manual wands give you control and better microfoam with practice, while automatic systems like the Bambino Plus texture milk for you — great if speed and consistency matter more than latte art.
