How to choose a cookware set

Material drives everything — performance, maintenance, and price. Here's how to pick the right one for how you cook.

Stainless vs. nonstick vs. cast iron

Tri-ply stainless steel browns best and lasts longest, making it the all-purpose choice. Nonstick is easiest for eggs and low-fat cooking but wears out over time. Cast iron sears like nothing else and lasts forever, but it's heavy and needs seasoning.

Look for fully clad construction

The best stainless sets are tri-ply or fully clad, meaning the aluminum or copper core runs through the entire pan, not just the base. That's what delivers even heating and prevents hot spots. Disc-bottom pans are cheaper but heat less evenly up the sides.

Buy the pieces you'll actually use

Big sets often pad the count with lids and pieces you'll rarely touch. A solid skillet, a saucepan, and a stockpot cover most cooking. Check oven-safe temperatures and handle comfort, and skip a 14-piece set if a focused 7-piece one fits your habits.
