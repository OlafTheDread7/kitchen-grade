How to choose a stand mixer

The right stand mixer comes down to how much power you need, the bowl capacity that fits your batches, and whether a tilt-head or bowl-lift design suits your counter. Here's what we weigh most.

Match power and capacity to your baking

For occasional cakes and cookies, a 5-quart tilt-head with 300–500 watts is plenty. If you regularly knead bread or bake in double batches, step up to a 6-quart-plus bowl and a 575-watt-or-higher motor so the machine isn't straining at its limit.

Tilt-head vs. bowl-lift

Tilt-head models are lighter, easier to store, and simpler to swap attachments on — ideal for most home kitchens. Bowl-lift designs are sturdier and built for heavy, sustained mixing, but they're taller and need more clearance under your cabinets, so measure before you buy.

Attachments and long-term value

The hub matters as much as the mixer. KitchenAid and Kenwood both offer pasta rollers, grinders, and spiralizers that bolt onto the front, turning one machine into a whole prep station. A wide attachment ecosystem stretches your investment far beyond mixing.
