How to choose an air fryer

The right air fryer comes down to capacity, basket style, and how much cleanup you're willing to do. Here's what we weigh most.

Match capacity to your household

A 3–4 quart basket is plenty for one or two people, but families should look at 5.8 quarts and up. Dual-zone models like the Ninja Foodi effectively double your capacity while letting you cook two foods at different settings.

Single basket vs. dual zone

Single baskets are simpler and cheaper, and they're easier to store. Dual-zone units shine when you want a protein and a side ready at the same time — just remember they take up more counter space and cost a bit more.

Cleanup and noise

Look for a nonstick, dishwasher-safe basket and a removable crisper plate. Quieter fans make a real difference if your kitchen is open to the living space, so we factor noise into every score.
